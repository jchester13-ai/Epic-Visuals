Note: If patient already has an active encounter, do not create a duplicate. Use "Transfer" workflow instead. Consult your Epic build team for multi-program scenarios.

# Dimension Severity (0–4) 30-Day Progress Clinical Rationale for Continued Stay

Resident Discharge Workflow — Six-Stage Sequence

Follow in order — each stage has a dependency on the previous
1. Pre-Discharge
2. Transition Planning
3. Documentation
4. State Reporting
5. Op. Closure
6. Hard Discharge
1
Stage 1 — Pre-Discharge
Verify Daily Notes & Care Plan Are Signed
Before initiating discharge, review the clinical chart to confirm all daily progress notes are signed and the current Care Plan is co-signed where required.
🛑 Hard Stop
Unsigned daily notes or an unsigned Care Plan will block the ability to complete a Hard Discharge. Resolve all outstanding signatures before proceeding.
All Daily Notes — Signed ✓ Care Plan — Co-Signed ✓ Outstanding Orders — Resolved ✓
2
Stage 2 — Transition Planning
Document Warm Handoffs & Follow-Up Appointments
Confirm and document all post-discharge arrangements. Each handoff should be documented as a communication in the chart.

🤝 Warm Handoff Checklist

  • Receiving program contacted by phone
  • Client introduced to next provider
  • Records transfer authorized (42 CFR)
  • Discharge summary sent/faxed

📅 Appointments to Confirm

  • OP SUD program intake date
  • PCP / MAT prescriber follow-up
  • Mental health continuation appt
  • Housing / benefits case manager
3
Stage 3 — Documentation
New Note: BHS SUS Discharge
Navigate to Assessment Docs → + New Note. Select the correct note type.
Note Type: "BHS SUS Discharge" *
📝 Note Contents
Include discharge disposition, final DSM-5 diagnosis, treatment summary, medications at discharge, follow-up plan, and client/family education provided. See Guide 8 for Discharge Plan vs. Discharge Summary distinction.
4
Stage 4 — State Reporting
Complete CalOMS Discharge Flowsheet
Navigate to the SUS Assessments Tab → open the CalOMS Discharge flowsheet. Complete all required fields for DHCS state reporting.
🛑 DHCS Mandate
CalOMS Discharge data is submitted to the California Department of Health Care Services. Missing or incomplete data may result in contract non-compliance. This flowsheet cannot be back-filled after Hard Discharge.
Discharge Date Discharge Reason Discharge Destination Substances at Discharge Employment / Living Status
5
Stage 5 — Operational Closure
Return Belongings & Close MAR
Complete operational discharge tasks. These must be done before Hard Discharge to prevent billing and medication reconciliation errors.
Client belongings inventory — returned & signed Valuables returned — receipt documented MAR — Closed / Medications reconciled Room/Bed — marked Available
6
Stage 6 — Administrative Hard Discharge
"Hard Discharge" — Closes the Admission Encounter
This is the final irreversible step. The Hard Discharge closes the residential admission encounter in Epic. Once completed, the patient is removed from the census and the encounter is locked.
⚠ Irreversible Action
Hard Discharge cannot be undone without IT/EHR admin intervention. Confirm all five prior stages are complete before clicking. This action closes billing, removes from census, and locks clinical documentation.
✓ Post-Discharge Outcome
Patient removed from residential census. Admission encounter closed. Bed marked available. Discharge paperwork available for print/download from Chart → Media Manager.

Discharge Plan vs. Discharge Summary

Two different documents — different purposes, different audiences, different timing
💡 Key Distinction
These are two separate clinical documents with different purposes. Confusing them leads to incomplete authorizations and regulatory deficiencies. Both may be required for a complete discharge.
📋 Option A — Discharge Plan
Forward-looking · Aftercare-focused · Auth Required
Perspective
Forward-looking. Describes what will happen AFTER the client leaves the program.
Primary Use
Required for every Reauthorization request (UM will not approve without it). Also used at actual discharge.
Core Content
  • ▸ Next level of care identified
  • ▸ Step-down program or facility named
  • ▸ Medication plan at discharge (MAT continuation)
  • ▸ Referrals made with dates
  • ▸ Follow-up appointments confirmed
  • ▸ Housing / transportation plan
  • ▸ Emergency contacts / crisis plan
Audience
BHS Utilization Management, client, receiving program
Timing
Updated at each authorization period and finalized at discharge
Epic Location
Reauth Order — Discharge Plan Section BHS SUS Discharge Note
🛑 Required for Reauth
A vague or missing Discharge Plan is the #1 reason UM returns reauthorization requests.
📄 Option B — Discharge Summary
Retrospective · Comprehensive · Clinical record
Perspective
Retrospective. Reviews the full residential stay from admission to discharge.
Primary Use
Clinical record, quality improvement, continuity of care communication. Not used for auth decisions.
Core Content
  • ▸ Admission date and presenting problem
  • ▸ Treatment milestones achieved
  • ▸ Clinical progress across ASAM dimensions
  • ▸ Medications during stay (including MAT)
  • ▸ Incidents / behavioral events
  • ▸ Diagnostic formulation at discharge
  • ▸ Reason for exit (planned / unplanned)
Audience
Receiving clinicians, primary care, legal/insurance requests (with valid ROI)
Timing
Completed at discharge only — not used during the stay
Epic Location
Assessment Docs → BHS SUS Discharge Note
⚠ Not a Substitute
A Discharge Summary does NOT replace the Discharge Plan for reauthorization. Both documents serve different functions.
Quick Decision Table
Situation Use Discharge Plan? Use Discharge Summary?
Submitting a reauthorization to UM ✅ Yes — Required ❌ Not used for auth
Client leaving the program today ✅ Yes — Finalize ✅ Yes — Complete
Sending records to a new treatment program ⚠ Include (aftercare plan) ✅ Yes — Primary document
Quality review / audit request Optional ✅ Yes
Mid-stay care plan update (not auth-related) ❌ Not applicable ❌ Not applicable
42 CFR Part 2 Reminder: Discharge Summaries containing SUD treatment information require a valid, signed ROI before release to any external party, including other health systems. Standard HIPAA TPO exceptions do not apply to 42 CFR Part 2 records.
